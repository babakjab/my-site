“Sevgi deşləndi, işıq daşlandı”

(Poem in Azerbaijani)

Sevgi nədir? Hıdırgama — işıq bəlkə səpmir

İşıq nədir? Sarsaqlama — sevgi bəlkə gəlmir

Daş nədir? Gürüldəmə — deş bəlkə dəlmir

Deş nədir? Sorgulama — daş bəlkə əzmir



Sevgi deşləndi — bəlkə də deşdi

İşıq daşlandı — bəlkə də daşdı



Deş daş oldu —

Dəl gəl oldu — ay! seç oldu

Sev daş oldu —

Gəl dəl oldu — ay! heç oldu



Sonunda —

Həm səpdi —

Həm də əzdi.
⸻

✍️ Author’s Commentary (English)


This poem rests on four elemental Azerbaijani words: sevgi (love), işıq (light), daş (stone), and deş (drill, pierce). Through natural verb forms like deşləndi and daşlandı, I test how meaning fractures when love pierces, when light hardens, when drilling itself becomes stone.



The poem draws on dialectal richness from both South and North Azerbaijani:

	•	Hıdırgama / Hıdırgamaq — a South Azerbaijani word, meaning muttering or babbling.

	•	Sarsaqlama — foolish or nonsensical speech.

	•	Gürüldəmə — the rumbling sound of something heavy.



By weaving these regional and oral words, the poem re-activates what is already inside the language but rarely used in literature. They are not invented neologisms, but rediscovered ones.



The only new coinage here is Deş (Drill) used metaphorically as the seed of the poem. This “drill” becomes an axis: love drilled, light stoned, the world collapsing into heç (nothing).



The movement runs from inquiry (Sevgi nədir? Işıq nədir?) to collapse (ay! heç oldu), ending with paradox: “both scattered, both crushed.”



Thus, the poem is more than wordplay. It is a linguistic excavation and a philosophical experiment — showing how Azerbaijani’s sound layers, dialectal textures, and verb forms can embody thought itself.

“Sevgi deşləndi, işıq daşlandı”

Preface


This is not a literal translation. The Azerbaijani original relies on sound, rhythm, and dialectal layers that cannot fully pass into English. What follows is a parallel rendering: each Azerbaijani line appears first, with an English line beneath it that captures the sense and philosophical movement rather than exact words.
⸻

Sevgi nədir? Hıdırgama — işıq bəlkə səpmir

What is love? Don't mutter — perhaps light does not scatter



İşıq nədir? Sarsaqlama — sevgi bəlkə gəlmir

What is light? Don't clown — perhaps love does not come



Daş nədir? Gürüldəmə — deş bəlkə dəlmir

What is stone? Don't rumble — perhaps the drill does not pierce



Deş nədir? Sorgulama — daş bəlkə əzmir

What is drilling? Don't question — perhaps stone does not crush
⸻

Sevgi deşləndi — bəlkə də deşdi

Love was drilled — perhaps it pierced



İşıq daşlandı — bəlkə də daşdı

Light was stoned — perhaps it overflowed
⸻

Deş daş oldu —

Drill became stone —



Dəl gəl oldu — ay! seç oldu

Madness came — ah! it became choice



Sev daş oldu —

Love became stone —



Gəl dəl oldu — ay! heç oldu

Come became madness — ah! it became nothing
⸻

Sonunda —

In the end —



Həm səpdi —

It both scattered —



Həm də əzdi.

And it also crushed.

Artist’s Note — Inside the Stone

Concept: Babak Jabbar Nezhad · Text: Resa (ChatGPT) · August 25, 2025

A single, immovable stone holds a hidden chamber. Inside, a young man leans into the rock, drilling—patient, precise, breathing against the weight. Behind him, a young girl plays the saz; the instrument is ringed with light, so that sound becomes illumination. Outside the stone, the sea rises and throws its waves from behind, testing the shell with every strike.
